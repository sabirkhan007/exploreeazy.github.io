AULI

Auli, a hill station in Chamoli District, is renowned for its Himalayan skiing resort. Surrounded by oak forests and the Nanda Devi and Nar Parvat mountains, Auli is also known as Auli Bugyals, which means "meadows" in Garhwali. A long cable car connects Auli to the town of Joshimath. As India’s premier ski resort, Auli was initially developed as a paramilitary base. Its skiing slopes are very popular, drawing many tourists and professionals. In winter, Auli becomes a hub for various snow adventure events, adding to its appeal as a top destination for winter sports enthusiasts.

NAINITAL

Nainital,known as the 'Lake District' is a beautiful hill station in Uttarakhand. Surrounded by seven hills—Ayarpata, Deopata, Handi-Bandi, Naina, Alma, Lariya-Kanta, and Sher-Ka-Danda—the town is famous for its emerald lakes, lush grasslands, ancient temples, and activities.Popular with honeymooners, Nainital paddle boating, horseback riding, hot-air ballooning, & mountaineering. Its serene ambiance and stunning landscapes a perfect spot for both relaxation & adventure, attracting visitors seeking a mix of tranquility & excitement.

BHIMTAL

Bhimtal is renowned for its picturesque lakeside setting, providing a peaceful retreat for tourists. This charming hill station in Nainital is celebrated for its natural beauty and tranquil atmosphere. During the monsoon, the lake fills completely, creating a stunning scene against the lush green hills. Adorned with quaint houses along the slopes, Bhimtal is ideal for a serene getaway. In winter, it transforms into a paradise for bird watchers, as migratory birds arrive, adding to the town's allure and making it a perfect destination for nature enthusiasts.

SATTAL

Sattal is a unique destination where seven freshwater lakes converge: Hanuman Lake, Ram Lake, Sita Lake, Lakshman Lake, Bharat Lake, Garuda Lake, and Nal Damyanti Lake. These interconnected lakes are surrounded by lush green scenery, adding to their charm. Renowned for its breathtaking beauty, Sattal offers picturesque landscapes and serene environments that attract nature enthusiasts and those seeking tranquility. The area is celebrated for its lush grasslands and stunning views, making it an ideal spot for relaxation and appreciating the splendor of nature. Its peaceful ambiance and natural allure make Sattal a sought-after retreat for visitors.

NEW TEHRI

New Tehri, the modern headquarters of the Tehri-Garhwal district, is a hub for adventure tourism. Overlooking the vast Tehri Lake and dam, this well-planned town attracts tourists with its natural beauty and water sports opportunities. Surrounded by temples, dense forests, and Himalayan peaks, New Tehri is an ideal vacation spot. It hosts various adventure events throughout the year, including the popular "Tehri Lake Festival." The Tehri Dam, the highest of its kind in India, is among the world’s largest hydroelectric projects, standing majestically on the Bhagirathi River.

RISHIKESH

Located about 24 km from Haridwar and nestled in the lower Himalayas, Rishikesh is surrounded by scenic hills on three sides with the holy Ganga flowing through it. Considered sacred, it is believed that meditation here leads to salvation. The area features numerous temples, both ancient and new, along the Ganges. Rishikesh is renowned as a major pilgrimage center related to the Ramayana and is known as the "Yoga Capital of the World" with many ashrams and yoga institutes. It also offers adventure activities like river rafting, kayaking, and cliff jumping, and serves as a gateway to Badrinath, Kedarnath, Gangotri, and Yamunotri.

DHANAULTI

Dhanaulti is located at an altitude of 2,250 m, it is settled very far away from the hustle bustle, busy market places and pollution. GARTANGALI

Gartangali is one of the most thrilling and exciting treks located in the beautiful Nelong Valley near the Indo-China border,in Uttarkashi district, The 150-year old Gartang Gali is a 136-m-long wood-lined stairway that is said to have been built by Peshawari Pathans, who created an architectural masterpiece by cutting the granite of a vertical rock face. This wooden bridge was the trade route between India and Tibet. The nomads (Bhotiya tribe) ferried their goods on yaks from this bridge for barter with their other country part. It was damaged over the years after it fell into disuse following the Indo China war in 1962. But Uttarakhand government decided to reconstruct it and finally it opened for the visitors and tourists after 59 years in 2021.

HARSIL

Harsil is known for its apple produce, Harsil apples are very popular all across the country. Harsil also provides many opportunities for adventure-seekers, such as trekking and hiking.

KANATAL

Kanatal is a Hill Station mainly famous for Himalayan Views, Weekend Retreat, Calm, Apple Orchids, and much more, making it an ideal spot for a peaceful getaway. Because of the excellent state of the roads, Kanatal is a safe spot to visit with family during the monsoon season, Visiting Kanatal during the monsoon is like feeling the love in the air since this is when nature’s splendor is at its most stunning. You can try meditation and nature photography as well. In winter, Kanatal receives snow and turns into a magical land.
